Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

The rocket man of India

The peoples president

Here's a timeline of his life:-

  • 1960 :- Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation
  • 1965 :- Started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1969 :- Received the government's approval and expanded the programme to include more engineers
  • 1970-1980 :- Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful
  • 1980 :- Directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme
  • 1992 :- Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation
  • 1998 :- Along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent"
  • 2002-2007:- Kalam served as the 11th President of India
  • 2012 :- Along with cardiologist Soma Raju, designed a rugged tablet computer for health care in rural areas, which was named the "Kalam-Raju Tablet"
  • 2015 :- Kalam was confirmed dead of a sudden cardiac arrest at the age of 83
